Drumbeat events bring together people with diverse skill-sets and passions, working to build a better web and world. Mozilla is all about shaping the future of the web for the public good. With Drumbeat, we're reaching out to new kinds of people – educators, filmmakers, journalists, scientists, artists – and coming together online and offline at face-to-face events around the world and at the annual Drumbeat Festival.

{{ _('Organize a local Drumbeat event') }}

Local, community-run events are a core part of the Drumbeat strategy. These events invite teachers, artists, filmakers, bloggers, lawyers, web developers and anyone else who cares about the internet to talk about building and protecting the open web for the future, and using open technologies to build a better world.

Mozilla is looking for local leaders who want to organize a Drumbeat event in their own city. To learn more, check out the Drumbeat Local Events kit on our wiki here: https://wiki.mozilla.org/Drumbeat/events

{{ _('The Mozilla Drumbeat Festival') }}

The annual Mozilla Drumbeat Festival is a big crazy event where 300 - 400 people gather yearly to talk about how their personal passion (like education, media or science) intersects with the open web. And make things. And have fun.

The 2010 Drumbeat Festival was focused on "Learning, Freedom and the Web" and brought together educators and hackers working to reinvent the future of learning. Their work continues online at http://learningfreedomandtheweb.org/. Or check out the 2010 Festival blog here: http://www.drumbeat.org/festival.
